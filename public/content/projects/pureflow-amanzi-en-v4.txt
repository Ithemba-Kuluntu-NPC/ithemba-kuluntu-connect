# 04. THE 6 STEPS OF TRANSFORMATION

# ------------------------------------------

# STEP 01: Structural Problem

step1.num: 01
step1.tag: Structural Problem
step1.heading: Women and children carry the heaviest burden
step1.text_block: In rural communities, unsafe water is also a gendered crisis. Women, grandmothers and teenage girls often carry the daily responsibility of collecting water, walking into forests to cut and carry firewood, boiling water at home and caring for children when waterborne illness affects the household. When children become sick, they miss school. Caregivers may have to travel long distances to clinics, often without enough money for transport. This creates many hours of unpaid labour, missed learning, fewer chances to seek paid work and deeper pressure on already vulnerable households.
step1.cta_label: Read the Full Picture
step1.image_path: /pureflow problem.png

# STEP 02: PureFlow Model

step2.num: 02
step2.tag: PureFlow Model
step2.heading: A full implementation platform, not a product handover alone
step2.text_block: PureFlow Amanzi combines household safe-water systems with WASH education, local assembly, community-based rollout, safe-use training, follow-up support and field data collection. The gravity-fed filter works without electricity, chemicals or boiling, using 0.1 micron membrane filtration to remove harmful bacteria from drinking water. The strength of the model is the delivery system around the filter: trained local teams, household onboarding, practical demonstrations, maintenance guidance and long-term follow-up that support correct use, trust and lasting behaviour change.
step2.cta_label: Explore the PureFlow Model
step2.image_path: /pureflow solution.png

# Step 02 Sub-Section: Loop Elements

step2.loop.heading: From first conversation to lasting use
step2.loop.sub_heading: The PureFlow model is a five-step delivery loop, designed around real rural conditions and the people who live there.
step2.loop.1.title: Listen
step2.loop.1.desc: Sit with local leaders and households. Plan together.
step2.loop.2.title: Assemble
step2.loop.2.desc: Filters built and tested by trained local hands.

step2.loop.3.desc: Hand-delivered into rural homes, even the hardest to reach.
step2.loop.4.title: Teach
step2.loop.4.desc: Practical WASH onboarding, household by household.
step2.loop.5.title: Stay
step2.loop.5.desc: Follow-up visits and community-based monitoring.

# STEP 03: Immediate Public Benefit

step3.num: 03
step3.tag: Immediate Public Benefit
step3.heading: Safe water reaches households, schools and ECD centres
step3.text_block: Immediate public benefit begins when households, early childhood development centres and rural schools receive access to safer daily water. PureFlow Amanzi pairs filtration with WASH education, safe-use demonstrations and practical training so families and educators understand how to use, clean and care for the system. This strengthens safe water habits from the start and helps protect children in the places where they live, learn and grow.
step3.cta_label: See Our ECD Footprint
step3.image_path: /pureflow school.png

# STEP 04: System Shifts

step4.num: 04
step4.tag: System Shifts
step4.heading: From urgent need to sustained household solutions
step4.text_block: True transformation happens when safe water becomes part of the household routine. Through WASH education, safe-use training, cleaning guidance, household demonstrations and follow-up visits, families learn how to maintain the system correctly and prevent recontamination after filtration. This shifts the response from short-term crisis coping to sustained household-level water safety, stronger uptake and long-term behaviour change.
step4.cta_label: Review Our Monitoring Framework
step4.image_path: /pureflow monitoring phone placeholder.png

step5.num: 05
step5.tag: Wider Community Gains
step5.heading: Safe water creates women’s livelihoods and wider community gains
step5.text_block: The delivery model converts structural water insecurity into paid local work opportunities for rural women and youth. Through assembly, rollout support, WASH facilitation, household follow-up and field data collection, women gain practical skills, income and leadership roles within their communities. At the same time, safer water can reduce illness-related clinic, medicine and transport costs, supporting better school attendance and easing pressure on household budgets.
