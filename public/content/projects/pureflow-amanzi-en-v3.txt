# 04. THE 6 STEPS OF TRANSFORMATION
# ------------------------------------------

# STEP 01: Structural Problem
step1.num: 01
step1.tag: The Crisis
step1.heading: Women and children carry the heaviest burden
step1.text_block: In deep rural areas, water insecurity is a gendered crisis. Mothers, grandmothers, and school-aged girls walk miles daily to collect water from muddy rivers and livestock dams. This unpaid labor costs thousands of hours annually, causes teenage girls to fall behind or drop out of school due to waterborne sickness, and exposes families to toxic wood smoke from open fire boiling.
step1.cta_label: Read the Full Picture
step1.image_path: /pureflow problem.png

# STEP 02: PureFlow Model
step2.num: 02
step2.tag: The Catalyst
step2.heading: A full implementation platform — not a product handover alone
step2.text_block: Dropping off loose parts does not solve a systemic crisis. The PureFlow model is a continuous, community-managed engine. The filtration bucket functions without electricity or chemical additives, filtering water down to 0.1 microns to completely eradicate bacterial contaminants.
step2.cta_label: Explore the Technical Solution
step2.image_path: /pureflow solution.png

# Step 02 Sub-Section: Loop Elements
step2.loop.heading: From first conversation to lasting use
step2.loop.sub_heading: The PureFlow model is a five-step delivery loop — designed around real rural conditions and the people who live there.
step2.loop.1.title: Listen
step2.loop.1.desc: Sit with local leaders and households. Plan together.
step2.loop.2.title: Assemble
step2.loop.2.desc: Filters built and tested by trained local hands.
step2.loop.3.title: Deliver
step2.loop.3.desc: Hand-delivered into rural homes — even the hardest to reach.
step2.loop.4.title: Teach
step2.loop.4.desc: Practical WASH onboarding, household by household.
step2.loop.5.title: Stay
step2.loop.5.desc: Follow-up visits and community-based monitoring.

# STEP 03: Immediate Public Benefit
step3.num: 03
step3.tag: Direct Relief
step3.heading: Transforming high-need learning environments
step3.text_block: Immediate change manifests within households, local early childhood development (ECD) spaces, and rural schools. When classrooms receive reliable, clean water, the risk of waterborne illnesses drops instantly. Children stay healthy, focused, and present in class, and school absenteeism declines dramatically, unlocking immediate learning potential.
step3.cta_label: See Our ECD Footprint
step3.image_path: /pureflow school.png

# STEP 04: System Shifts
step4.num: 04
step4.tag: Sustained Action
step4.heading: From urgent needs to permanent household solutions
step4.text_block: True transformation happens when an entire community changes its long-term habits. By introducing a reliable, simple cleaning protocol (backwashing via specialized syringes/nozzles), families are fully empowered to maintain their own systems indefinitely. The traditional mindset shifts from crisis-coping to permanent health security.
step4.cta_label: Review Our Monitoring Framework
step4.image_path: /pureflow monitoring phone placeholder.png

# STEP 05: Wider Community Gains
step5.num: 05
step5.tag: Economic Upliftment
step5.heading: Safe water creates independent female livelihoods
step5.text_block: The delivery model converts structural water insecurity into formal, paid employment opportunities for rural women and youth. By learning manufacturing standards, supply tracking, and data-driven field auditing, women step into prominent leadership and coordination roles within their villages. This creates stable, green incomes that are directly reinvested into household food security, children's health, and local trade.
step5.cta_label: Explore Our Green Jobs Model
step5.image_path: /pureflow local women jobs.png

The community establishes a strong grassroots platform capable of anchoring future developments—from local agricultural initiatives to advanced educational facilities.
